CAMBRIDGE

History and Highlights

Nestled along the serene River Cam, Cambridge is a city steeped in history, boasting the prestigious University of Cambridge founded in 1209. This scholarly haven is adorned with architectural gems such as the iconic King's College Chapel and the historic Bridge of Sighs, creating a captivating tapestry of academic legacy and timeless beauty that enchants visitors from around the globe.

Punting

Punting along the tranquil River Cam is an essential Cambridge experience, where visitors can leisurely glide on flat-bottomed boats guided by skilled punters using long poles. ... As you drift past the picturesque college Backs and under charming bridges, punting provides a serene and quintessentially Cambridge way to absorb the city's rich history and stunning architecture.

Botanical Garden

The Cambridge University Botanic Garden is a verdant oasis, spanning 40 acres and offering a diverse collection of over 8,000 plant species. ...Established in 1762, this botanical haven not only showcases the beauty of meticulously curated landscapes but also serves as an educational hub, providing insight into plant conservation and biodiversity. Visitors can meander through themed gardens, explore glasshouses featuring exotic flora, and revel in the peaceful ambiance that makes the Cambridge Botanic Garden a horticultural treasure in the heart of the city.

Cambridge Market

Cambridge Market is a bustling and vibrant hub nestled in the heart of the city, offering a delightful array of stalls and vendors. ... From fresh produce and local crafts to international cuisine and unique finds, the market provides a sensory feast for visitors, creating a lively atmosphere where locals and tourists alike can immerse themselves in the diverse offerings and vibrant energy of this historical marketplace.

How to Get Here

Approaching Cambridge by car, the M11 motorway provides a direct route from London, offering a smooth and efficient journey. Alternatively, those coming from the north can access Cambridge via the A14, connecting the city to major road networks and ensuring convenient access from various directions.

Cambridge is well-connected by train, with regular services from London King's Cross taking approximately an hour. Additionally, trains from various cities across the country, including Birmingham and Norwich, provide convenient and efficient access to this historic city.

Cambridge does not have its own commercial airport; however, travelers can access the city via the nearby London Stansted Airport, which is approximately 30 miles away. From Stansted, visitors can utilize various transportation options, including trains and buses, for a straightforward journey to Cambridge.
